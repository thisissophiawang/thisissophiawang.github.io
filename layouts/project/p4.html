Project overview

ElektraFi is a financial technology platform that transforms personal finance management into an engaging and rewarding experience. The project focuses on implementing a gamification framework that motivates users to build healthy financial habits through structured challenges, streaks, and achievement badges.

The Financial Challenges feature serves as the central pillar of the gamification strategy, allowing users to participate in various financial tasks and track their progress over time. The platform reinforces positive financial behaviors through points, badges, and visual feedback.

Implementation Process

1

Research & Concept Development

Analyzed fintech competitors and gamification best practices to identify effective engagement mechanisms and opportunity areas. Developed concept prototypes for the gamification system focused on financial health.

2

UI/UX Design

Created intuitive interface designs in Figma for the "Tasks" and "Financial Challenges" pages, with emphasis on clear progress visualization, achievement displays, and challenge cards.

3

Frontend Development

Implemented the designed UI using TypeScript/TSX and SCSS, creating responsive components for challenge cards, progress indicators, badges, and streaks counters.

4

Backend Integration

Extended the backend (Node.js, Python) to support challenge tracking, streak calculations, points accumulation, and badge award logic. Implemented APIs for challenge completion verification.

5

Testing & Optimization

Conducted user testing to validate engagement metrics and refined challenge difficulty levels, point allocations, and visual feedback based on user response data.

The A/B testing project revealed a transformative approach to understanding user engagement within a fintech platform targeting corporate employees. By systematically comparing user behavior before and after the introduction of the gamified Task Page, we uncovered critical insights into platform adoption dynamics. The experimental design allowed us to move beyond anecdotal evidence, providing quantitative proof of the intervention's effectiveness.

Our most compelling finding was the potential shift in user interaction patterns. The comparison between pre and post-implementation metrics highlighted the power of targeted design interventions. By carefully tracking registration rates, time spent on the platform, and user satisfaction, we were able to construct a nuanced understanding of how gamification can potentially overcome barriers to digital platform adoption.
